As you may know the non-native killer shrimp has now been confirmed in the Trinity Broads.

Killer shrimp is so called due to its voracious habits: eating or killing any of our native invertebrates small enough to be prey. Growing to 3cm long, adults will feed on fish eggs and fry as well.

In order to minimise/slow the spread of this non-native species it is imperative that all anglers take the following steps

  • Check your gear after leaving the water for mud, aquatic animals or plant material. Remove anything you find and leave it at the site.
  • Clean everything thoroughly as soon as you can, paying attention to nets, waders, and areas that are damp and hard to access. Use hot water if possible.
  • Dry everything for as long as possible before using elsewhere as some invasive plants and animals can survive for two weeks in damp conditions.

More information about killer shrimp and other non-native species is available via the Non-Native Species Secretariat.

Please note that Bridge Inn Fishery have adopted a new booking system and the Club will be using this system on behalf of our members when you book this venue.

To help simplify the end-to-end booking process we would be grateful if members please check availability at Bridge Inn BEFORE emailing NUAC your booking request.

To do this please go to BIF Bookings (this system can also be accessed via the Shop button on BIF Facebook).

Scroll to the bottom of the page to see a calendar which shows availability across the three BIF venues (Main, Pleasure & River). Select the Show Month option to show availablity by day. Day cells that are not greyed out indicate swims are available. If you are using a PC/Laptop you can place your cursor over a cell to display a message indicating how many swims are still available.

Assuming the days you require are not greyed out then please email bookings@nuac.org.uk indicating your chosen BIF Venue, Date & Time.

When a booking is confirmed the associated email we send you will include three pdf attachments: An Invoice, The Fishery Rules and a BIF Permit for your visit. You MUST have this Permit with you when you fish at Bridge Inn (either on your phone or printed off beforehand); this will enable the bailiff to confirm your booking.

This is a new system for those of us on the Bookings Roster. Please be patient as there may be teething problems while we familiarise ourselves with this new process. May we also remind members to provide at least 24 hours notice (from the start of your planned visit) otherwise we cannot guarantee your booking will be processed on time.

Please note that Shallowbrook Lakes have refreshed their fishery rules.

Key changes are:

  • Lure fishing is restricted to Snipe Lake and only during November through to February.
  • No live baiting.
  • No braid mainline.
  • Safe rigs only - assume no fixed rigs, please contact the fishery for further clarification if required.

  • Unhooking mats must be used for all catches.
  • Landing net must be large enough for your target species.

NUAC are very pleased to announce we have secured access to two stretches of the River Wensum at Drayton.

From the start of the river season members can access the majority of the northern bank sandwiched between Taverham Mill and Costessey Mill - nearly 2.5km of fishing accessed via grazed fields. A further 750m of river running below Drayton Low Road, accessed from the southern bank is also available. This stretch is a little more wild with it being accessed over un-grazed land.

Your Committee has been keen to obtain access to a non-tidal river stretch and hope members see this as a valuable and different addition to the choice of fishing on offer.
